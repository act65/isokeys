Hexboard

An isomorphic keyboard you play in the browser

Play with your computer keyboard (rows 1–0, Q–P, A–;, Z–/) or click the hexes. Every chord shape is the same anywhere on the grid — that's the isomorphic magic.

Every key has 6 neighbours. On an isomorphic layout the step to each one is the same everywhere on the grid — change the layout or the step sizes above and watch these intervals change. (Centre key: —.)

The highlighted hexagons are the chord's shape — slide that exact shape anywhere and it's the same chord in a new key.

Each step clockwise is a perfect fifth — the interval that drives most chord progressions. Neighbours here sound closely related; opposite sides clash.

A scale is a contiguous arc: pick major/minor and watch 7 adjacent notes light up. Pentatonics are a shorter arc; whole-tone / diminished break into a pattern. The same notes glow on the keyboard. Click a note to hear it and re-centre.
